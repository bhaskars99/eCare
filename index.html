Eyes

  • With aging the lens stiffens making it hard to focus on close objects

    Require magnifying reading glasses

  • The lens become denser and it makes seeing in the dim light harder

    Brighter light is needed for better reading

  • The eye produce less fluid making the eyes dry

Ears

Most changes in hearing due to loud noise exposure But some changes in hearing are just due to aging, regardless of loud noise exposure. It becomes really difficult to hear the loud-pitched voice.

Mouth and Nose

In the 50’s mostly the sense of taste and smell starts diminishing and taste buds on tongue decrease in sensitivity. This change affects sweet and salt more than bitter and sour.

Brain

The number of nerve cells decreases in the brain. With age number of nerve cells may lose some of the receptors for messages. Older people may react to and do tasks slowly. The ability to learn new and recall the words decreases.

Shoulder

Most common problems of shoulders are

  • Tendon inflammation
  • Instability
  • Arthritis
  • Fracture

In case of acute injury seek a medical attention as soon as possible. But if the pain is less severe try to rest for few days to see if time fixes the problem.

Hand, Wrist and Finger

A sprain is a most common injury. This is often caused by a fall onto a outstretched hand. A sprain could also happen during daily activities.

Symptoms
  • Cannot put weight on or straighten a hurt limb, or a joint wobbles or feels unstable.
  • Severe pain.
  • Welling and pain do not improve after 2 days of home treatment.
Treatments
  • Use rest, ice, compression, and elevation (RICE) for pain and swelling.
  • Treat hands sensitive to cold by avoiding and protecting your hands from the cold.
  • Avoid sleeping on your hands, which may decrease blood flow to your fingers.
  • Treat blisters on fingers or hands.
  • Stop, change, or take a break from your activities.

Elbow/Arm

Bursitis occurs when a bursa becomes inflamed. People get bursitis by overusing a joint. It can also be caused by an injury. It usually occurs at the knee or elbow. Kneeling or leaning your elbows on a hard surface for a long time can make bursitis start. Doing the same kinds of movements every day or putting stress on joints increases your risk.

Symptoms

Elbow/arm bursitis include Bruising, redness, or warmth around your elbow, pain and swelling

Treatments

    Start R.I.C.E. measures soon after your injury.

  • Rest : Take a break from your normal activities.
  • Ice : Place an ice pack or bag of frozen peas on the sore area for 15 to 20 minutes three times a day.
  • Compression : Use a compression bandage to reduce swelling.
  • Elevation : If possible, elevate your arm to help reduce swelling.

Knee

Knee pain has many causes. A relatively common cause of knee pain in older men and women occurs when a segment of bone loses its blood supply and begins to die.

Symptoms
  • Sudden pain on the inside of the knee, perhaps triggered by a specific activity or minor injury
  • Increased pain at night and with activity
  • Swelling over the front and inside of the knee
  • Heightened sensitivity to touch in the area
  • Limited motion due to pain
Treatment
  • Medications to reduce the pain
  • A brace to relieve pressure on the joint surface
  • A conditioning program with exercises to increase the strengthen of the muscles in your thighs
  • Activity modifications to reduce knee pain

Legs

Playing sports, running, falling, or having an accident can damage your legs. Common leg injuries include sprains and strains, joint dislocations, and fractures. ertain diseases also lead to leg problems. For example, knee osteoarthritis, common in older people, can cause pain and limited motion.

Symptom

Is there a deformity of your leg, or are you unable to stand or put pressure on your leg?

Treatment

See your doctor or go to the emergency room right away. Apply ice to the affected area.

Symptom

Is there bruising or swelling of your calf or ankle?

Do you have pain or mild swelling in the front or inner part of your lower leg that may have started after physical activity such as running or jumping?

Treatment

Get plenty of rest and avoid activities that cause pain. Use an anti-inflammatory medicine such as ibuprofen and apply ice to the area. See your doctor if pain or swelling gets worse or doesn't get better.

Foot

Symptoms

  • Bunions

    A bony growth or misaligned bone at the base of the big toe or sometimes on the small toe.

  • Medications

    Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), such as aspirin or ibuprofen, or corticosteroid (cortisone) injections may help to relieve pain

  • Calluses and corns

    Creams or petroleum jelly (Vaseline) – regular use can soften dead skin

    Cotton or other soft material placed between your toes to prevent them from rubbing against each other

  • Hammertoes

    apply soft padding between the affected toes, and wear wide shoes or custom-made orthotic inserts or footwear. Exercises and splints may help.

  • Toenail problems

    If the nail is infected, by a bacteria or fungus, you will require antibiotics. You may have to take antibiotics both by mouth and also apply antibiotic cream or antifungal ointments on the toe.

  • Heel Pain

    Moderate, gentle walking, swimming, or bicycling is beneficial, but do not overdo it.

    For more severe cases, night splints, leg supports, corticosteroids (cortisone shots) and local anesthesia may be needed for plantar fasciitis. However, these approaches are not used for heel spurs.

Heart Disease

Heart disease remains the leading killer of adults over age 65, accounting for 489,722 deaths in 2014. As a chronic condition, heart disease affects 37 percent of men and 26 percent of women 65 and older, according to the Federal Interagency Forum on Aging-Related Statistics. As people age, they're increasingly living with risk factors, such as high blood pressure and high cholesterol, that increase the chances of having a stroke or developing heart disease. Dr. Bernard’s advice for addressing this senior health risk not only helps with heart disease but can improve senior health across the board: “Exercise, eat well, get a good night’s rest. Eating well means eating in a fashion that will allow you to keep a healthy weight with a well-balanced and healthy diet."

Symptoms
  • Chest pain (angina)
  • Shortness of breath
  • Pain, numbness, weakness or coldness in your legs or arms if the blood vessels in those parts of your body are narrowed
  • Pain in the neck, jaw, throat, upper abdomen or back
Prescription
  • ACE inhibitors: These widen arteries to lower your blood pressure and make it easier for your heart to pump blood
  • Aldosterone inhibitors: Eplerenone (Inspra) and spironolactone (Aldactone) are part of a class of medicine called diuretics.

Respiratory Diseases

Chronic lower respiratory diseases, such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), are the third most common cause of death among people 65 and older, with 124,693 deaths in 2014, according to the CDC( Centers for Disease Control) . Among people 65 and older, about 10 percent of men and 13 percent of women are living with asthma, and 10 percent of men and 11 percent of women are living with chronic bronchitis or emphysema, according to the Federal Interagency Forum on Aging-Related Statistics. Although having a chronic respiratory disease increases senior health risks, making you more vulnerable to pneumonia and other infections, getting lung function tests and taking the correct medication, or using oxygen as instructed, will go a long way toward preserving senior health and your quality of life.

Symptoms
  • Dyspnoea
  • Cough
  • Chest pain
  • Wheeze
Prescription
  • Accolate : Treatment for asthma
  • Adcirca (tadalafil): For the treatment of pulmonary arterial hypertension
  • Arcapta (indacaterol maleate inhalation powder) : For the treatment of airflow obstruction resulting from chronic obstructive pulmonary disease

Diabetes

The CDC( Centers for Disease Control) estimates that 25 percent of people ages 65 and older are living with diabetes, a significant senior health risk. According to CDC data, diabetes caused 54,161 deaths among adults over age 65 in 2014. Diabetes can be identified and addressed early with simple blood tests for blood sugar levels. The sooner you know that you have or are at risk for diabetes, the sooner you can start making changes to control the disease and improve your long-term senior health outlook.

Symptoms
  • Frequent urination
  • Excessive thirst
  • Increased hunger
  • Weight loss
  • Tiredness
  • Lack of interest and concentration
  • A tingling sensation or numbness in the hands or feet
  • Blurred vision
  • Frequent infections
  • Slow-healing wounds
  • Vomiting and stomach pain (often mistaken as the flu)
Prescription
  • Acarbose : is used along with a proper diet and exercise program to control high blood sugar in people with type 2 diabetes.
  • Tanzeum : can be used alone or added to existing treatment regimens to control blood sugar levels in the overall management of diabetes
  • Glyburide : used with a proper diet and exercise program to control high blood sugarin people with type 2 diabetes.

Obesity

Obesity is an important senior health risk factor for heart disease, diabetes, and cancer — all chronic conditions that impact quality of life. As the numbers on the scale increase, so does the risk for disease. Of the adults between 65 and 74, 36.2 percent of men and 40.7 percent of women are obese — meaning that their body mass index is greater than or equal to 30 — according to the CDC( Centers for Disease Control) . It can also be a signal that an older adult isn’t as active or mobile as he or she once was.

Symptoms
  • Clothes feeling tight and needing a larger size.
  • The scale showing that you've gained weight.
  • Having extra fat around the waist.
  • A higher than normal body mass index and waist circumference.
Prescription
  • Phentermine : Appetite suppressant; anorectic
  • Orlistat :Lipase inhibitor; inhibits fat absorption in the intestine.
  • Lorcaserin :Selective serotonin 2C receptor agonist; promotes a feeling of fullness or satiety

Immune System

The cells of the immune system act more slowly. These cells identify and destroy foreign substances such as bacteria, other infecting microbes, and probably cancer cells. This immune slowdown may partly explain several findings associated with aging:

  • Cancer is more common among older people.
  • Vaccines tend to be less protective in older people.
  • Some infections, such as pneumonia and influenza, are more common among older people and result in death more often.
  • Allergy symptoms may become less severe.

As the immune system slows down, autoimmune disorders become less common.

Symptoms
  • Fatigue
  • Frequent Infections
  • Flu, Colds, Sore Throat
  • Allergies
  • Injuries that Take Long to Heal
Prescription
  • ABREVA (docosanol): Cold sore/fever blister.
  • Albenza (albendazole):Treatment for parasitic infections caused by worms
  • Allegra-D:Treatment for nasal congestion due to seasonal allergy

Mental Health

Sometimes referred to as psychiatric or psychologic disorders, mental health disorders are caused by complex interactions between physical, psychologic, social, cultural, and hereditary influences. They involve disturbances in thinking, emotion, and/or behavior. Small disturbances in these aspects of life are common, but when such disturbances distress the person greatly, interfere with daily life, or both, they are considered mental illness or a mental health disorder. The effects of mental illness may be long-lasting or temporary.

Symptoms
  • Withdrawal — Recent social withdrawal and loss of interest in others
  • Drop in functioning — An unusual drop in functioning, at school, work or social activities, such as quitting sports, failing in school or difficulty performing familiar tasks
  • Problems thinking — Problems with concentration, memory or logical thought and speech that are hard to explain
  • Increased sensitivity — Heightened sensitivity to sights, sounds, smells or touch; avoidance of over-stimulating situations
  • Apathy — Loss of initiative or desire to participate in any activity
  • Feeling disconnected — A vague feeling of being disconnected from oneself or one’s surroundings; a sense of unreality
  • Illogical thinking — Unusual or exaggerated beliefs about personal powers to understand meanings or influence events; illogical or “magical” thinking typical of childhood in an adult
  • Nervousness — Fear or suspiciousness of others or a strong nervous feeling
  • Unusual behavior – Odd, uncharacteristic, peculiar behavior
  • Sleep or appetite changes — Dramatic sleep and appetite changes or decline in personal care
  • Mood changes — Rapid or dramatic shifts in feelings
Prescription
  • Fluoxetine : Antidepressants
  • Citalopram : Antidepressants
  • Sertraline : Antidepressants
